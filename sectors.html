Investing in Specific Sectors

Sector ETFs allow investors to gain targeted exposure to specific industries or segments of the economy, as defined by classification systems like GICS. This can be used to overweight sectors you believe will outperform, hedge other portfolio exposures, or gain access to industries not well-represented in broad market indexes.

However, sector investing comes with **concentration risk**. By focusing on a single industry, these ETFs can be much more volatile than diversified broad market funds, and their performance is heavily tied to the fortunes of that specific sector. It's crucial to understand the specific companies and drivers within a sector before investing. Use the tool below to compare ETFs within various sectors.

Key Differences & Considerations (Sector ETFs)

When choosing Sector ETFs, consider these factors:

  • Concentration Risk: This is the biggest factor. Sector ETFs concentrate investment in one industry, making them inherently riskier and more volatile than broad market ETFs. Performance is tied to that sector's specific economic conditions, regulations, and cycles.
  • Geographic Focus: Some sector ETFs focus purely on Canadian companies (e.g., XIT, ZEB), while others offer global or US exposure from a Canadian listing (e.g., TEC, ZUH, ZMT). Understand the geographic scope.
  • Index Methodology: Check if the ETF is market-cap weighted (larger companies have more influence, like XFN, XEG) or equal weighted (gives smaller companies the same influence as larger ones, like ZEB, ZEO). Some may track specific sub-sectors (e.g., XGD for gold miners within Materials).
  • MERs: Sector ETF MERs vary more widely than broad index or asset allocation ETFs, often ranging from ~0.30% to over 0.60%. Compare costs carefully.
  • Specific Structures: Look out for swap-based structures (like HXT, HXE, HXQ) for potential tax efficiency but added complexity/risk, or leveraged ETFs (like HCAL) which amplify returns and risks.
  • Overlap: Be mindful of potential overlap if holding multiple sector ETFs or sector ETFs alongside broad market funds.

Sector investing requires more research and active monitoring than broad diversification strategies. Ensure any sector bet aligns with your overall portfolio goals and risk tolerance. Always check the ETF Facts document.
